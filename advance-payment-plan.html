Advance Payment & Yearly Subscription System

Plan Document — Prepared for Client Review

1. Why This Feature?

Currently, a student can only pay after a monthly due is generated. There is no way to handle:

  • A student who wants to pay early (e.g., June's fees in May)
  • A student who wants to pay yearly at a discounted rate

Two real scenarios:

Scenario 1 — Advance: A student wants to pay June's fees in May. There is no "June" due yet, so the system cannot accept the payment.

Scenario 2 — Yearly Subscription: A student wants to pay the whole year at once (12 × 1,000 = 12,000 Tk) and you offer a discount: "Pay 10,000 Tk for the entire year." The system has no way to track this package or apply the monthly discount automatically.

2. The Solution: Student Advance Balance

Instead of forcing every payment to match a specific month's due, each student gets an Advance Balance — a prepaid wallet that holds their credit.

TermMeaning
Advance BalanceMoney the student has paid in advance, sitting as credit on their account
Auto-ConsumeWhen a new monthly due is generated, if the student has advance balance, the system automatically uses it to pay
Zero-Due RuleA student can only add advance money when all their past dues are fully cleared

Example — Simple Advance (Monthly)

Student: Rafiq wants to pay June's 1,000 Tk fee in May.

Step 1: Staff checks — Rafiq has no unpaid dues from previous months. ✓
Step 2: Staff enters an Advance Payment of 1,000 Tk.
Step 3: System records this as an earning and adds 1,000 to Rafiq's advance balance.
Step 4: In June, when dues are generated, the system sees Rafiq has 1,000 advance balance. His June due is 1,000. The system automatically matches them.
Step 5: June's due shows as Paid, advance balance becomes 0.
Result: Staff sees a clear record: "Paid via advance on 15-May-2026."

3. Yearly Subscription — Handling the Discount

This is the trickiest case. A student's batch fee is 1,000 Tk/month. The yearly total is 12,000 Tk. But you offer a deal: pay 10,000 Tk and cover the whole year.

If you simply add 10,000 to the advance balance, the system would consume 1,000 each month. By month 10 the balance runs out — but the student effectively paid only 833.33/month. We need a smarter approach.

How It Works

ConceptHow It Works
Yearly Package Staff enters: Batch = SSC Batch, Coverage = 12 months (June 2026 – May 2027), Paid Amount = 10,000 Tk
Effective Monthly Rate System calculates: 10,000 ÷ 12 = 833.33 Tk/month
Bulk Discount Each month the system notes: "Full due is 1,000, package rate is 833.33. A 166.67 discount is applied."
Auto-Consume System deducts 833.33 from the advance balance each month

4. Step-by-Step Examples

Example A — Student pays June's fee in May

May 15 — Staff opens student profile. System checks: all previous dues are paid. ✓
Staff clicks "Record Advance Payment" → enters 1,000 Tk → note: "Advance for June"
Earning created. Advance balance = 1,000 Tk.

June 1 — System generates monthly dues. Student has 1,000 advance balance.
System auto-pays the June due. Due shows: Paid (via advance). Balance = 0.

Example B — Student pays yearly with discount

Student says: "I want to pay for the whole year (June 2026 – May 2027). Full price 12,000, I'll pay 10,000."

Step 1: Staff creates a Yearly Package:
  • Batch: SSC 2026 Batch
  • Coverage: 12 months starting June 2026
  • Paid Amount: 10,000 Tk
  • Reference: "Yearly Subscription 2026-27"

Step 2: Earning created for 10,000 Tk. Advance balance = 10,000 Tk.

Step 3 (June): Due generated: 1,000 Tk. Bulk discount applied: 166.67 Tk. Net due = 833.33. Advance deducted: 833.33.

Step 4 (July through May): Same pattern. After 12 months → package completed. Balance = 0.

Example C — Student with half-year payment

Student pays 5,000 Tk for 6 months (half year).
Batch fee = 1,000/month. Half-year total = 6,000.

Effective monthly rate = 5,000 ÷ 6 = 833.33 Tk/month

Months 1-6: Due = 833.33 (after 166.67 bulk discount)
Month 7 onwards: No more package → reverts to full 1,000/month
Unused balance: Refundable or adjustable.

5. The "Clear Dues First" Rule

Enforced everywhere — before recording any advance or yearly payment:

Check: Does the student have any Unpaid or Partial dues?

If YES ❌ → Show error: "Please clear all outstanding dues first"
If NO ✓ → Allow advance / yearly payment

This prevents confusion where a student has old unpaid fees but also credit.

7. Handling Edge Cases

RiskSolution
Student leaves mid-year after yearly paymentRemaining balance is refundable. Staff issues a refund entry; balance returned to student.
Student changes batch mid-yearProrate the remaining balance — either transfer to new batch or refund.
Fee amount increases mid-yearYearly package honored at the agreed rate. Difference absorbed as additional discount.
Fee amount decreases mid-yearPackage rate adjusted downward; remaining balance lasts longer or is partially refunded.
Wrong coverage period enteredStaff can edit the package if no dues have been consumed yet. If consumed, requires prorated adjustment.
Discount stacking (bulk + per-student)Both discounts add together. Total discount never reduces due below 0 Tk.
